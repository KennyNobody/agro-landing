WHAT WE DO?

Kalagra group grows, distributes and harvests a bioenergy crop known as “Miscanthus x Giganteus”, biomass of which is then used as a feedstock to produce biofuel pellets or cubes.


Whilst most biomass pellets are derived from trees and forest operations recent studies have proven that, given demand, this source of biofuel is unsustainable in the medium and long- term, trees simply don’t grow quickly enough and therefore cannot be relied on as a long-term solution.

Our objectives

To develop a new source of sustainable fuel in the Russian Federation for both domestic and export utilisation

To provide permanent employment to more than 120 people in the rural areas of Kaliningrad

To create this opportunity in the Kaliningrad Region taking advantage of the geographical proximity to Western Europe and the favourable climatic conditions for agriculture

To utilise difficult and often unviable agricultural land and bring it into production in line with Russian Federal objectives

What is Miscanthus?

  • Sustainable perennial energy crop
  • C4 Asiatic grass ‘elephant grass’
  • Planted once, yields biomass annually
  • Dries naturally
  • Ecologically beneficial
  • 20 + year lifespan

What effect on land and environment

  • Sterile crop that cannot self propagate
  • Low mineral and nitrogen requirement so will grow on substandard land
  • Thrives in high water content soils and can survive flooding
  • No chemical inputs or fertilizer once established
  • No known diseases, pests or predators
  • Soil fixer & builder due to mulching of leaves and fine deep root structure
  • Easy to remove if land required for alternative use

Miscanthus as the fuel

  • Annual harvest of dry biomass up to 20 tonne /Ha
  • Below 15% water content at harvest
  • Easy and efficient to pelletize
  • 4444 kwh/tonne (virgin wood = 4800kwh/t)
  • Will burn in the same way as wood pellets
  • 2-3% ash that can be used as a direct fertilizer

About Kalagra group

The Kalagra group of companies is an integrated holding of both Russian and UK companies intended to take advantage of the vertical opportunities offered in bioenergy growth, production and utilization in the Kaliningrad Region, Russian Federation.

Kalagra Limited is the UK holding company, used as the investment vehicle for investment outside the Russian Federation. Kalagra OOO is the overall Russian holding company and acts as the management company for three Russian subsidiaries: Kalagra Farm OOO, Timo Land OOO and Timo Invest OOO.


Kalagra Farm is the agricultural company dealing with the main production processes:



  • clearing and renovation of land;
  • planting and development of Miscanthus and other bioenergy crops;
  • harvesting and logistics of delivery of biomass;
  • development and management of the Miscanthus nursery and sale of rhizomes;
  • production of biofuel in cubes and pellets from Miscanthus and other biomass mainly for export and to the local market;
  • agronomy consultancy both to our own companies and ultimately to external farmers

Timo Land OOO and Timo Invest OOO are ringfenced companies intended to own or lease land to own companies and other companies in the Kaliningrad region and invest into the production of Miscanthus or other biofuel crops.

  • Formed four limited companies based in Kaliningrad to manage the different elements of the business
  • Have six highly skilled full-time employees working on the project: General Director, ommercial Director, Operations Manager, Chief Accountant, Interpreter and Office Manager, Property Director, Environmental Manager. Total potential employment in the project is more than 150 people with the majority from unskilled or semiskilled rural locations
  • Already invested in 400 Ha of agricultural land in the Chernyakhovsk region and have agreed the purchase of a further 1300 Ha and eventually our goal is to have a pool of 10 000 Ha of agricultural land
  • Have ready and waiting a supply of Miscanthus rhizomes for the spring of 2019 for the initial 20 Ha required to establish the nursery production

  • Agreed contract to supply biofuel pellets under a fixed price twenty-year indexed agreement to the United Kingdom. Total export potential of this agreement is up to 8.4 billion RUB
